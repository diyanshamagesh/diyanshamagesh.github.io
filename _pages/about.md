I'm a Psychology graduate from the University of California, Davis, with hands-on research experience in clinical and neurodevelopmental psychology. I'm passionate about understanding the emotional and behavioral underpinnings of neurodevelopmental conditions — and building research that makes mental health care more accessible to adolescents and youth.

My core research experience is at the **UC Davis MIND Institute's AIR Lab**, where I support two active studies — **Q-DATA** and **VRAM** — investigating attention and distractibility in adolescents aged 8–18. My work spans facilitating VR research sessions, supporting clinical intake interviews, maintaining IRB-compliant data integrity, and collaborating across multidisciplinary teams.

Beyond the lab, I've worked as a **Clinical Psychology Intern** at Roshini Trust in Hyderabad, India (2023, 2024), assisting licensed clinicians with standardized assessments including the DST and VSMS, and observing behavioral interventions for children with neurodevelopmental disorders. I also conducted longitudinal behavioral observation of preschool-aged children at the **Peregrine School** in Davis.

Outside of research, I mentor students with Autism Spectrum Disorder and other disabilities through UC Davis's **Student Disability Center**, and serve as a **California Young Adult Ambassador** for Soluna, promoting mental health awareness among college-aged communities.

I'm proficient in **REDCap** and **Qualtrics**, hold CITI certifications in social/behavioral research and GCP, and am fluent in **English, Hindi, Telugu, and Tamil**. I'm currently seeking Research Assistant or Junior Analyst roles as I build toward a Ph.D. in Clinical Psychology.
