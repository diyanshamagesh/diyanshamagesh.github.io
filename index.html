About

I'm a Psychology graduate from the University of California, Davis, with hands-on research experience in clinical and neurodevelopmental psychology. I'm passionate about understanding the emotional and behavioral underpinnings of neurodevelopmental conditions — and building research that makes mental health care more accessible to adolescents and youth.

My core research experience is at the UC Davis MIND Institute's AIR Lab, where I support two active studies — Q-DATA and VRAM — investigating attention and distractibility in adolescents aged 8–18. My work spans facilitating VR research sessions, supporting clinical intake interviews, maintaining IRB-compliant data integrity, and collaborating across multidisciplinary teams.
